Words do not automatically convey their intended meaning. We (the listeners – listening to others as well as listening to ourselves) act on the meaning we ourselves make, which is not necessarily the one intended. Therefore, we need a set of questions to clarify ambiguities.
When we express an idea in language there are three possible outcomes.
- We generalize from a particular example as if it represents all possibilities.
- We delete or ignore facts.
- We distort situation by giving descriptive words a meaning they do not support.
The tool has been developed to ask a series of questions designed to unravel language to avoid misunderstandings. It clarifies communication by prompting the user / problem solver to fill in important missing information, reshape the language if it is misleading, and connect generalities with specific examples. Obtaining the right answer depends on asking the right question.
As you progress, this tool will ask you a series of questions to clarify such ambiguities and help state your problem summary sentence more clearly.
